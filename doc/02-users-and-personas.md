# JoinJoy — Users and Personas

## 1. Primary User Types
### 1.1 Host / Bill Creator
The person who pays first or takes responsibility for creating the event and adding expense items.

### 1.2 Participant / Member
The person who joins the event and claims what they had.

## 2. Core User Needs
### Host needs
- a fast way to create the event
- a way to add itemized expenses
- a way to avoid assigning everything manually
- confidence that totals are fair
- an easy way to collect money

### Participant needs
- an easy way to identify their items
- transparency on calculation
- a simple way to pay
- confidence they are not overpaying

## 3. Jobs To Be Done
### Functional jobs
- When a group eats together, help us split uneven costs fairly.
- When one person pays first, help the group settle later.
- When there are shared dishes, help us split them correctly.
- When service charge and VAT exist, help us distribute them fairly.

### Emotional jobs
- Help me avoid awkward payment conversations.
- Help me feel that the split is fair.
- Help me avoid the feeling that I am doing admin work.
- Help me close the night smoothly.

### Social jobs
- Help the group coordinate without confusion.
- Help the host look organized, not controlling.
- Help participants feel included in the process.

## 4. Persona A — The Organized Host
### Profile
- Age: 28–40
- Often the one who pays first
- Comfortable creating the event
- Wants structure, but not complexity

### Goals
- create the event quickly
- avoid manually deciding who owes what
- collect money without follow-up chaos

### Pain points
- existing apps feel too complicated
- guests do not respond quickly
- shared dishes are hard to assign
- tax/service charge logic is unclear

### Quote
“I’m okay paying first, but I don’t want to become the accountant of the group.”

## 5. Persona B — The Cooperative Friend
### Profile
- Age: 24–38
- Wants to pay fairly
- Does not want the host to handle everything
- Comfortable using a mobile web app

### Goals
- quickly claim own food and drinks
- understand how the total is calculated
- pay the host with minimal friction

### Pain points
- unclear item lists
- no way to claim shared dishes correctly
- total feels random when tax/service charge is added
- difficult or ugly UI

### Quote
“Just let me choose what I had and tell me how much to transfer.”

## 6. Persona C — The Casual Group Member
### Profile
- Not very detail-oriented
- Wants the flow to be extremely simple
- May join late
- May not care about every detail but still wants fairness

### Goals
- get in, claim, and be done
- avoid reading too much
- trust the host and the app

### Pain points
- too many buttons
- accounting language
- confusion around estimated vs final total

### Quote
“Please don’t make me think too much. I just want to pay my part.”

## 7. Main Scenarios
### Scenario 1 — Dinner with shared dishes
A group of 6 eats Thai food with many shared dishes.
The host enters the items.
Each member joins and claims what they had.
Shared dishes are split among selected people.
SC and VAT are allocated proportionally.

### Scenario 2 — Bar tab
A group orders drinks.
Some drinks belong to one person, some pitchers are shared.
One person paid.
The group joins the event and claims drinks.

### Scenario 3 — Office lunch order
Team members join a lunch event.
Each person claims their meal.
Delivery fee or extra fee can be treated as a shared item.

## 8. Key Design Implications
- host setup must be fast
- participant claim flow must be even faster
- language must be social, not accounting-heavy
- item-level actions must be clear
- unresolved items must be easy for host to fix
- final totals must be trustworthy
